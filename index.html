Long Stretch of Gravel

Kirikiriroa is a beautiful city just 1.5 hours south of Auckland in New Zealand. The Māori name for Hamilton translates to “long stretch of gravel”. With a few staple destinations, The Hamilton Gardens, Hamilton Zoo, and Hamilton Lake, there is plenty of nature to explore. There are over 12 designated walkways and paths cutting through and wrapping around the city.

Did you know that Hamilton is one of the only cities in New Zealand to support a resident population of long-tailed bats? You might just hear some echoing around Hamilton Lake or The Waikato River if the conditions are right!

Walking in Nature Can Benefit Our Health

Spending time in green space or bringing nature into your everyday life can benefit both your mental and physical well-being. Breathing fresh air can help regulate your levels of serotonin and promote happiness and well-being. The recommended distance we should be walking each day is 10,000 steps. Walking this range of steps in a day has a wide range health benefits:

  • Improve your mood
  • Reduce feelings of stress or anger
  • Help you take time out and feel more relaxed
  • Improve your physical health by burning body fat and strengthening muscles
  • Strengthen the heart and increase circulation.
  • Help you meet and get to know new people
  • Connect you to your local community
  • Help you feel more connected to nature

Tis the Season to be Spooky

Kirikiriroa is a great place to explore, however not very many know how truly different it is at night. If you're feeling brave enough and invite a few friends too, there are a few walks waiting to be explored...

Every so often the Hamilton City Gardens will open up at night and you can re-explore all your favourite gardens. You can also travel to the Hamilton Lake but it can get quite dark and spooky... don't say I didn't warn you...
