Hamilton Gardens

Throughout history, gardens have been a way of expressing the important philosophical ideas of their time. In some respects the story of the gardens reflects the story of civilisation and attitudes to the natural world. The collections of gardens at Hamilton Gardens explore the history, context and meaning of gardens. Each tells a story and offers an insight into a different culture. Located in the middle of Hamilton, this is a popular tourist attraction the world over.

Walk Type: Loop

Total Length: 2.1km

Average Time to Walk: 75 minutes or 35 minutes at non-stop pace

Accessibility: Easy but some gardens have stairs which makes it difficult to get around.

Location: Hungerford Crescent via State Highway 1C, Hamilton, 3216

Newstead Walkway

Newstead Walkway is an easy out and back trail just outside of Hamilton that is ideal for families with children. Dogs are also allowed, if they are on a lead. The walk features forest, a stream and a creek, wooden bridges, rural scenes and a large clearing surrounded by towering eucalyptus trees which is a perfect picnic spot. The pathways, a mix of gravel, dirt, grass and boardwalks, are well established and mostly protected from the elements.

Walk type: Out and back

Total length: 2.3km return

Average time to walk: 35 minutes

Accessibility: Easy (weather dependent)

Location: State Highway 26 between Hamilton Park Cemetery and Newstead Model School or 126 Vaile Road

Lake Ngā Roto

Ngā Roto is a significant cultural site of importance to iwi and a treasured local community asset just north of Te Awamutu. The boardwalk at Lake Ngā Roto is open to walking, running and cycling activities. Dogs are welcome on a lead. The boardwalk wraps around a natural wetland surrounding the lake and is regularly used by the local rowing club, sailing club, duck hunters, walkers and is a popular tourist attraction.

Walk Type: Loop

Total Length: 5.9km

Average Time to Walk: 1 hour

Accessibility: Very Easy

Location: 65 Bank Road, Te Awamutu 3883

Taitua Arboretum

Taitua Arboretum is home to an impressive selection of mature trees spread across 22 hectares. Featuring lakes, woodland gardens, bird-life and ample picnic spots, all linked by a network of walking tracks. The arboretum is perfect for a leisurely weekend stroll, family picnic, or a more active outing. Dogs are welcome within the arboretum but must be kept on a lead at all times. The track is hilly with a range of surfaces like dirt and gravel which may be difficult for less abled people to manage.

Walk Type: Loop

Total Length: 2.3km

Average Time to Walk: 40 minutes

Accessibility: Medium

Location: 55 Taitua Road, Temple View, Hamilton 3289
